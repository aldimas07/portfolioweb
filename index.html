    My Fixed Mindset

    My fixed mindset and the reason why I wrote that:

    I don't like to debate or argue

    - I prefer transquility (When people are arguing in a forum and things get heated, I think this is bad)

    - I am afraid of hurting people (When expressing opinions or denying someone's arguments in a meeting, it will hurt that person's heart)

    - I think I'd better keep silent (When other people argue and their arguments look good, I guess I'll just have to sit back and watch)

    - Opinions in my head sometimes cannot be conveyed properly or in line with what I say (I sometimes take too long to compose the words that I will say from my mind, so that when I speak my words are sometimes different from what I thought before)

    I don't like talking in front of the public

    - I lack self-confidence I feel the audience who listens are people who are smarter than me

    - I am a stiff person I don't know how to bring a pleasant atmosphere

    - I often get nervous when I speak When speaking, I often lose sight of what I'm talking about

    - I am afraid that my words will get out of control I am afraid that if I speak intentionally or unintentionally, consciously or not, I may offend certain parties

    - When I speak, I am afraid that I will not be as cool as other students I saw that other students were better at choosing words and presenting the atmosphere

    Challenge to change from fixed mindset to growth mindset:

    1. I don't like debate or argue: Debating doesn't always end in conflict, to fight for what you think is right with the support of reference sources and logical reasons is not a problem but by practicing continuously to convey and refute arguments, debating for a long time is not be a daunting thing, but to arrive at a solution that works for all parties involved.

    2. I don't like to talk in front of the public: I have to master the material that I convey when I will speak in public. What I can do is look for references on the topic to be discussed, divide it into small parts about the points I will convey so I don't forget, and try to practice speaking alone in front of the mirror. By doing this, I think the results will certainly be better.

    3. I can't be disciplined to wake up in the morning: I have to have a target every day to end all activities before 11 pm. I can start exercising regularly so that at night I can go to sleep soon. Besides that, I will also try not to use gadgets 1 hour before going to bed.
